The brain is 2-5% body weight, but consumes 20% of the energy 

- When you eat carbs, they’re broken down into glucose in the small intestine.
- Glucose crosses intestinal cells and enters the capillaries
- Glucose is water-soluble, so it dissolves directly in the **plasma**. Plasma is 90% water, with some amount of glucose, electrolytes, hormones, and proteins. 

* **Cerebral blood flow**: How much blood is flowing into the brain (millileters per gram of tissue per minute)
* **Cerebral blood volume**: How much blood is in the brain


* There are surrounding non-neuronal cells (glial cells etc) that detect when there has been neuron spiking and release signals to relax the arterial wall to induce more blood flow into that region of the brain

| Method            | Physical signal         | What it actually measures              | Direct neural activity? | Spatial resolution | Temporal resolution | Practical notes |
| ----------------- | ----------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------- | --------------- |
| **EEG**           | Electric potential (µV) | Summed post-synaptic potentials        | Yes                     | Low–moderate       | Very high (ms)      | Cheap, portable |

EEG is measuring the difference in voltage between one of the the electrodes and another (reference) electrode placed somewhere else on the scalp. The signal measured by EEG is roughly the average post-synaptic potential of huge numbers of neurons.

EEG is very surface level -- there is absolutely no way to penetrate deeper than roughly where you place the electrode. 

#### Functional Ultrasound

- Neural activity increases local metabolic demand
- Blood flow increases within ~100 ms
- fUS detects **~1% changes in blood volume**
- functional ultrasound uses the doppler effect -- the blood is moving relative to the wave source 
- Red blood cells are the major scatterers
