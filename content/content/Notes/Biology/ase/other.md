* Ionic: electron transfer
* Covalent: electron sharing. Electrons are closer because of this. 
	* In water or biological environments, ionic bonds are dramatically weakened. High dielectric constant of water screens charges, reducing the bond dissociation energy (energy required to break the bond). In isolation, ionic bonds are extremely strong because the particles exert coloumb forces on each other. 
* Hydrogen bond: polar interactions
* Van der Waals: nonpolar interactions
