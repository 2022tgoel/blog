* **Muller's Ratchet**: since any mutations are more likely to harm than help an individual, the offspring is on average less fit that the parent. However, generating diversity through recombination makes the expected fitness of the child the same as the parent. 
* Like you can have a lower mutation rate and still make the same amount of evolutionary progress because if one individual acquires good gene X and another individual acquires good gene Y than they can share the genes with each other and get a super individual with good genes X and Y. Otherwise, a single individual needs to accumulate all of the mutations.
