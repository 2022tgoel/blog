* B-cell antigen receptor = antibody = immunoglobulin (Ig)
	* Symmetric polypeptide chains, heavy chain and light chain
		* both membrane-bound and can be secreted later 
	* three complementarity determining regions (CDRs) -- high variablility regions of the polypeptide chain 
	* Each B-cell expresses one and only one antibody, and it has unique antigen
	* **somatic recombination** of the genes generate diversity for the antibodies (you don't have enough real estate in the genome itself to encode all of them)
* T-cell receptor (TCR)
	* always membrane bound
	* just two chains of polypeptides

### Memory
These antibodies have memory, such that 
1. The lag to produce antibodies is shorter
2. the concentration is larger
3. the fit (affinity) of the antibodies is stronger
the second time you generate an immune response to a foreign substance.
