Originally this post ended here, because I was mostly trying to make sure I remember this story structure after I failed for the second time to write a complete plot in my story :/. But let me actually say a bit more about writing stories. Obviously, this is a very helpful formula for create an interesting story. But you don't have to follow it; I barely have in my stories. For example, you could write a story about someone going on a walk, and they're just in their own head thinking about random stuff, and it could still be really engaging. Writing has no *strict* rules, only things that work (and they might only work for some people!) and things that don't.  

Why should you try creative writing? 

Well, I think there are a few points: 

* It's really fun! Just try it. 
* I think it's hard to understand how much your writing is a representation of *you* until you try it. Ultimately, all of your writing is motivated by something you have experienced that you think is worth communicating to others, or just thinking more about. You could argue that you are trying to write something that is enjoyable to read, and you are solely optimizing for other people's reactions, but I don't think anyone really writes like this. You probably care, care *a lot*, about the characters and the plot. Sharing your writing with a someone can genuinely give them a better sense of who you are, and I honestly think it can be more effective than / very complementary to just talking to them, even if only because you put a lot of effort into the piece and the effectiveness of communication scales with effort.
* I also think that creative writing can improve your clarity of thought process, even in other areas of writing such as technical writing. My professor talks about how "writing is the process of translation" of the thoughts in your mind onto words on a page. How many times have you started to speak and then forgotten the right word for what you wanted to say? Or had an argument with someone and realized you were talking about completely different ideas with the same words (taboo)? I think a lot through language (my "internal monologue"), and I qualitatively feel that writing more improves the efficiency of this monologue.
