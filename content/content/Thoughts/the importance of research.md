
Working on research for almost a year now has caused to have some thoughts on what research is actually impactful. At the beginning, I was very results oriented: is something is state-of-the-art, that's pretty impactful, right? It's about what works. However, I don't think this is true. Something working, and people using it, can be very temporary, especially in the field of machine learning where things change so fast. It really matters that your research stands the test of time, that people look back at it in 10 years (an ML timeline) and still think it was important. If something is $\epsilon$ closer to something *fundamentally correct*, and people can appreciate that this is true (which might mean getting good results...), that's what actually matters. There so many papers out there being published that push the state-of-the-art. But I encourage you to think about whether they feel *principled* and *fundamental*.
