Your feelings/intuitions can be really incorrect. 
Examples:
* You've been trying to get an idea to work, because you've been working on it for so long you develop a sense of "attachment" / sunk cost fallacy and become adamant that this is the correct idea and waste more time 
* You really enjoyed a conversation with someone at a company, so now you want to work there. They're probably not super representative of everyone you'll be working with.
* Everyone else around you wants x, so you want x

Once you realize this, you tend to obsessively rationalize everything you do and not trust your instincts. 

When I started writing this, I intended on talking about how there's still a place for intuitions in decisions that are hard to rationalize. But actually, I don't know how to deal with decisions that are hard to rationalize. There are a lot of extremely important decisions that are like this! Where to work, who to spend time with, etc. I generally try to talk to a lot of people about such decisions and mull over them for a long time. If anyone has any other strategies please let me know!
