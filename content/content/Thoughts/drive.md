
There are several elements that fuel your drive.

* **Mastery**: You want to be the best you can possibly be. It's quite natural to enjoy doing something you are good at. You love the process of self-improvement. This also can be competitive: you probably wouldn't consider yourself a master unless other people also did. I find that mastery doesn't actually work that well for me, since I tend to get rather insecure about my skillset.   
* **Curiosity**: You want to get to the bottom of how things work. You are excited by fundamental questions about the world, and you will do whatever it takes to find the answer. I find I do my best when motivated by curiosity. 
* **Impact**: You want to change the world. You want your life to have meaning, whatever that means, your actions to amount to something, and you think it is up to you to make sure that is the case. 

If you talk to someone with a lot of drive, I think 1-2 of these factors tend to dominate the others. Something interesting to think about.
