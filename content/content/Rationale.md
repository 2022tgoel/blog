Very inspired by [Alek Westover's Blog](https://awestover.github.io/thoughts)

My previous blog was not very successful, mostly because it was a lot of effort to maintain. There are a couple of appeals of this new format:

* I'll (hopefully) write more
* Obsidian seems cool! If I like it more than Notion that's pretty positive, if I don't maybe this blog will die
* You make writing public for the people interested in reading it. One concern with posting half-baked thoughts is that no one will be interested in reading it, so you might as well keep it to yourself. However, personally, I've felt that it's not hard to skip over a bunch of poor quality material, and that it's interesting to read even the incoherent thoughts of my friends. I spend a lot of time talking with my friends, and on average my spoken word makes a lot less sense than my written word. Yet, we still really enjoy the conversations. 
* I like the idea of having timestamps for my entries and being able to easily view them in a linear log. It helps me see the evolution of my mental headspace over time.
* I can probably just `.gitignore` things that I don't want to be public

I think the concept of a stream-of-consciousness blog is really interesting, because it gives people a lot more insight into how your brain works than a normal blog.
